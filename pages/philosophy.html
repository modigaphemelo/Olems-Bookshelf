Existential Meaning

Explorations of life's purpose and the human condition, grappling with suffering and mortality.

12 Books

The Denial of Death by Ernest Becker

Overview

How our terror of mortality drives all human civilization. The ultimate buzzkill that somehow makes you feel better about your existential dread.

Being and Time by Martin Heidegger

Overview

The book that made philosophy students question their life choices. Dasein, thrownness, and being-toward-death made unnecessarily complicated.

Sartre's attempt to make existentialism sound optimistic. We're condemned to be free, which is either terrifying or liberating depending on your mood.

The Plague by Albert Camus

Absurdism • Allegory

Overview

A pandemic as metaphor for the human condition. Surprisingly relevant no matter what century you're reading it in.

At the Existentialist Café by Sarah Bakewell

Existentialism with a side of croissants and drama. The philosophers were almost as interesting as their philosophies.

Irrational Man by William Barrett

A guided tour through existential thought that somehow makes Kierkegaard and Nietzsche seem like reasonable people.

Notes from Underground by Fyodor Dostoevsky

Existentialism • Psychology

The original incel manifesto written by a literary genius. Depressing, brilliant, and uncomfortably relatable in small doses.

Despair analyzed with the cheerful optimism only a Danish philosopher can muster. Not recommended for your beach reading list.
